Design Process

Logo design

Client: an eco-friendly salon called Tameable needed a new logo.

Step 1

  • Brainstorm
  • Connecting physical with abstract ideas.
  • If I can't visualize what I want, then I'll sketch on paper or research.
Who are they?
A zero waste spa and salon, offering bulk supplies hair & skin care.

Who are their customers?
The DIY crowd, who love craft workshops, buy natural, organic products, herbs, essential oils, live without plastics, reusable and sustainable.

Design notes: wild vs tame
plain vs sparkly
painterly/calligraphy?

What happens at the salon?
before: wild/crazy/unmanagable
during: wet, bundled
after: dry, clean, sleek tamed

Step 2

Initially, I designed a swirl of calligraphy. Elegant and flowing like a strands of hair.

Step 3

Refine my ideas.

I realized the "brush" wasn't the right direction. Instead I turned to the image of curly hair, before the salon tames it.
Using the Scribble effect in Illustrator, I adjust the mess to look deliberate.

Step 4

Finalizing the design, I propose bright eco-friendly colors and present it on different backgrounds.

Creative Code / Digital Art

Creating an algorithmic art sketch

I begin the research phase by studying other examples of algorithmic art. To simplify, I decided on rotation and triangles as my theme. Most of the early static designs resemble flowers.

Randomness - the key element!
It represents the randomness of life. The sketch include random colors values. The movement occurs in tiny out of control increments. Each rotation is unique. Each new pinwheels leaf appears onto the black screen unexpected and without end.

By embracing the chaos you accept that you have lost control, and gain peace of mind.

The Coding Process
My approach to coding is slow, taking one tiny step at a time.

First, I created a rotating triangle. Then I added constraints that move the triangles around the screen. They were a single red color until I finally change the colors. Triangles appearing on the right of the screen gradually turn yellow.

The code complexity builds until the final step.

My final step is to organized my thoughts. I finish my documentation. I hope you enjoyed summer pinwheels.
